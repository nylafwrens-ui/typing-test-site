Typing Lessons Beginner Friendly

Short lessons to build good habits: accuracy first, speed later.

Lesson 1: Home Row & Finger Placement Place your index fingers on the F and J keys (they usually have small bumps). Keep your other fingers resting on A–S–D–F and J–K–L–; with thumbs on the spacebar. Return to this position after every key press.
Lesson 2: Accuracy Before Speed Type slowly and focus on hitting the correct keys. It’s better to type 20 WPM at 98% accuracy than 50 WPM at 70%. Speed will naturally increase as your fingers learn the patterns.
Lesson 3: Short Daily Practice Practice 10–15 minutes a day instead of one long session once a week. Use the practice section to warm up, then take a short test to measure progress.
Lesson 4: Gradual Difficulty Start with easy sentences, then move to medium and hard. Harder text usually has punctuation, numbers, or uncommon words. Don’t rush—move up only when you feel comfortable.
